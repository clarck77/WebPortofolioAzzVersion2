DREAMS

Since I was first introduced to the world of technology, I have always been fascinated by how the internet works. Every website I visited, every interactive feature I used, always made me wonder: "How was all this created?" From that curiosity, a big dream grew inside me—to become a skilled web developer who can create digital solutions that are not only functional but also beneficial for many people. However, I don't want to stop there. For me, a great web developer is someone who keeps learning and growing. The world of technology moves fast, and I want to be part of that change. I aspire to become a DevOps engineer who not only understands application development but also ensures that those applications run smoothly, efficiently, and at scale. I want to master the integration between development and operations, combining the two to create reliable and resilient systems. Looking even further, my ultimate dream is to step into the role of a technical architect in a large company. I want to be the mind behind designing complex technology systems, building infrastructures that not only meet current needs but are also ready to face future challenges. For me, a technical architect is not just a designer but also a visionary—someone who can see far ahead, understand business needs, and translate them into the right technological solutions. I believe that becoming a reliable professional is not just about technical skills but also about having vision and commitment. This dream may seem ambitious, but I trust that every small step I take will bring me closer to it. From learning to code, understanding DevOps principles, to honing my skills in system architecture design, all of these are part of my long journey toward this goal. Technology is a world full of endless opportunities, and I want to be among those who use these opportunities to create something meaningful. I don’t just want to be a developer, engineer, or architect that people rely on, but also someone who can inspire and make a positive impact on many people through my work.
